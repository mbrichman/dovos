5. Document Security Change Auditing

Control Reference: AU-3 - CONTENT OF AUDIT RECORDS


Control Language: "Audit record content that may be necessary to satisfy the requirement of this control, includes, for example, time stamps, source and destination addresses, user/process identifiers, event descriptions, success/fail indications, filenames involved, and access control or flow control rules invoked."

Federal Web Application Requirements (GSA AU-2): "Web applications should log all administrator activity, authentication checks, authorization checks, data deletions, data access, data changes, and permission changes."

Current State Gap: Our platform only indicates that document security settings have changed but does not log:

Previous security state of documents

Specific changes made to security settings

User who made the security modifications

Detailed change descriptions

This violates GSA's explicit requirement for web applications to log "data changes" and "permission changes" with sufficient detail for audit purposes.

Regulatory Impact: This creates insufficient audit trails for compliance requirements and incident investigation capabilities. The NetDocuments colleague emphasized that ACL snapshot capabilities were "deal-breakers" for their largest FedRAMP customer, making this a critical compliance requirement.

Competitor Comparison:

Box: Box Reporting feature provides audit trails for viewing activity on folders, showing who accessed information and when (Box User Activity Report)

NetDocuments: Provides comprehensive document ACL history logging for all document/container objects, including person/group names, unique IDs, and specific access changes (NetDocuments Document History). Critical Enhancement: Since April 2022, NetDocuments' Consolidated Activity Log (CAL) captures the entire ACL before and after every change, enabling precise point-in-time ACL reconstruction (NetDocuments CAL Documentation). This capability was specifically developed to meet the "minimum acceptable requirements" of their largest FedRAMP customer and is considered essential for federal compliance

ND experience

While working with a critical government agency over a multi-year timeframe the customer "stressed multiple times" that ACL snapshot capabilities were non-negotiable and threatened to go to another service if the requirement was not met. This underscores the critical importance of comprehensive audit logging for federal compliance.

Industry Standard Implications: NetDocuments developed their enhanced Consolidated Activity Log specifically to meet these federal requirements, capturing complete before/after states of all ACL changes since April 2022. The fact that competitors including ND and Box have invested significantly in these capabilities demonstrates that our current gaps represent fundamental blockers to federal market entry.
